Setting

The world is a dangerous, chaotic place always in need of adventurers.Your character lives at the Hold, a massive city surrounded by towering walls, a melting pot of every race and occupation. This bastion of civilization led by a council of noblemen is the only real city left aside from villages and towns scattered across the land. The city of the Hold and the surrounding lands are vast, designed to be fluid and flexible. You have the creative liberty to invent whatever other people, places, and organizations you want to fit your character’s story. You could even stop reading right here and not worry about missing much! Keep reading if you’d like to learn more about the city of Hold, the surrounding lands, and some of the well-known organizations your character might run into or be a part of.

The Hold

The Hold itself is an enormous city protected by massive walls. Within the city are all kinds of areas and districts, including the following:

-The Markets. Busy streets full of taverns, inns, stores, smiths, and workshops.

-The University District. Clusters of schools, guildhalls, and scholarly organizations.

-The Temple District. Sections dedicated to churches, temples, and monasteries.

-The Groves. Areas of trees and plant life where those close to nature live. -The Noble District. Boulevards of noble estates and manors.

-The Suburbs. Avenues of cottages, houses, and other residencies.

-The Slums. Run-down streets and unsafe back alleys.

-The Underdungeon. Underground networks of ancient tunnels, buildings, and caves.

At the center of the city is City Square where the massive Grand Tower of the council stands, visible for miles abroad. From there, the city’s main streets, known as the Straights,run outwards to each of the city’s main Gates.

The Mainland

The Mainland River docks, villages, and farmland surround the city. Beyond these are a wide variety of biomes and environments, including the following:

-The Mainland Fields. Expansive grassy fields that surround the Hold for miles abroad.

-The Fey touched Forest. Verdant forests to the south.

-The Inner Sea. Ocean waters to the west, connected to the Holdby rivers.

-The Rotting Marsh. Putrid swampland to the south, between the sea and forests.

-The Dragon Teeth Mountains. Rugged mountains that stretch across the north and east.

-The Frostlands. The frigid tundra further north than the northern mountains.

-The Sunscorched Savannas. The Dry Grasslands Beyond The Eastern Mountains.

-The Tombsman Desert. The rocky ravines and vast, sandy expanses past the savannas.

Organizations

You have the creative freedom to create whatever organizations fit your character's story, but you can also use these well-known ones shown below.

The Companions

A guild of new and seasoned adventurers alike who fight as protectors of the people and strive to achieve dreams, find a new life, and help others do the same.

The Grand Council

A huge council of noblemen that govern the Hold and the Mainland, led by twelve prime counselors (non-player characters) in handling political affairs and national emergencies.

The City Watch

A city-run policing force of guards, investigators, soldiers, and watchmen who work tirelessly to keep the peace and defend the Hold. Within the City Watch is the Vanguard, a more specialized group often requisitioned to handle specific threats, both within the Hold and across the Mainland.

The Sage's Guild

An organization of knowledge-seekers who wish to preserve civilization through studies, invention, and the collection of relics. They have amassed a tremendous amount of knowledge, consisting of all varieties of science and magic, the planes of existence, and their inhabitants.

The Shepherd's Eye

A group of wardens devoted to preserving and protecting the old ways of the wilds, balancing the peace between nature and civilization. Many barbarian clans, ranger conclaves, and druid circles ally under this group.
